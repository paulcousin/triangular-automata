Rule 53

Rule 53 is one of the most beautiful and interesting of all.


Chaos

Rule 53 is chaotic. If we take two randomly generated grids that are completely similar except for the central cell, which is alive in (1) and dead in (2), the trajectories strongly diverge

(1) at $t=0$ (2) at $t=0$
(1) at $t=512$ (2) at $t=512$

Steady states

A stroboscopic universe alternating between fully alive and fully dead is a weakly unstable equilibrium of rule 53. It can remain stable if disturbed just slightly. However, a bigger or unlucky perturbation will make it fall into one of the two stable equilibria, at least locally.

Equilibria of rule 53

The stable equilibria are states where all the triangles in one phase (e.g pointing to the left) are alive and the others dead. These cristaline states can have impurities in the form of oscillators. A mask is applied in second half of the following video to reveal these phases.
