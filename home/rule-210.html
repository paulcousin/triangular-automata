Rule 210

Rule 210 is easy to describe: “cells with one living neighbor change state”.

Starting from a single living cell, it creates a very large stable structure with an intricate shape. This structure starts at a distance of about 1160 from the origin. It is drawn by a difference of density between two regions, both of which are stable up to some oscillators.

Rule 210 initially alternates between what seems like two global population densities (i.e. number of living cells / size of the first cell's region of influence). When the new structure appears, around $t=1024$, the population stabilizes in a density cycle of period 4 between slowly evolving values.

The origin triangle must remain alive due to the symmetries of this rule. If we look at the second center column, we see it starts repeating when the center of the grid stabilizes in an oscillator of period 14 at $t=5592$.
